Welcome to FAQs

Heart FAQs is dedicated to providing accurate and reliable information about heart health, common heart conditions, prevention tips, and about our tool. Our goal is to empower individuals to take control of their heart health by offering comprehensive answers to frequently asked questions.
FAQ

Common questions

Here are some of the most common questions that we get.
What is the purpose of this website? The purpose of this website is to allow medical staff to input various medical parameters and predict the likelihood of a patient having heart problems.
What values are required to be input for prediction? The values required for prediction include age, sex, chest pain type, resting blood pressure, cholesterol level, fasting blood sugar, resting electrocardiographic results, maximum heart rate achieved, exercise induced angina, ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest, slope of the peak exercise ST segment, number of major vessels colored by fluoroscopy, thalassemia type.
How accurate are the predictions made by this website? The accuracy of predictions is based on the performance of best model selected. The Random Forest model used for prediction has been trained on a dataset and may provide estimates based on that data with the accuracy of 85.29%.
Is the input data secure and confidential? Yes, all input data provided by medical staff is secure and confidential. We prioritize data privacy and protection.
What is heart disease? Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart rhythm problems, and heart defects.
What are the risk factors for heart disease? Risk factors for heart disease include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, and a sedentary lifestyle.
How can I prevent heart disease? You can prevent heart disease by maintaining a healthy diet, exercising regularly, not smoking, managing stress, and getting regular check-ups with your healthcare provider.
What are the symptoms of a heart attack? Symptoms of a heart attack can include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, nausea, lightheadedness, and pain or discomfort in other areas of the upper body.
How is heart disease diagnosed? Heart disease can be diagnosed through various tests such as electrocardiograms (ECG), echocardiograms, stress tests, cardiac catheterization, and blood tests to measure cholesterol levels and other markers of heart health.
How can I get in touch for further inquiries or support? For further inquiries or support, please go to Contact Page and reach out to our team. Our team will be happy to assist you.
